# Profile

This file is the evaluative frame for your briefing. `/focus` reads it and judges your tasks against the role that matches the context you ask for. The more concrete it is, the sharper the prioritisation.

Copy this file to `me/profile.md` and replace the contents with your own. `profile.md` is gitignored, so your personal version stays out of the repo.

## Work role

What you're responsible for at work, what you're optimising for, and what good looks like. The briefing uses this to decide which work tasks matter today.

Example: I'm a senior designer. I'm optimising for influence over outcomes, not volume of output. Tasks that shape a decision early count for more than tasks that polish work already decided.

## Personal role

The role you're showing up for outside work, and what matters in it.

Example: I'm optimising for being present and reliable at home, not for personal productivity for its own sake. A task that protects time with the people I live with outranks one that just clears the list.
